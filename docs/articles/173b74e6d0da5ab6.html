Overview

Migrating to the cloud was supposed to make infrastructure easier to provision and manage. For many enterprises, the reality is more complicated. New pain points emerged that undermine the cloud’s speed, security, and scale.

Technical breakdown

A centralized, event-driven knowledge graph, Infragraph provides the unified visibility that keeps the focus where it belongs: empowering organizations to secure and optimize infrastructure.

In this blog, we’ll outline why Infragraph is a must-have resource for platform teams as they combat the ever-growing challenges of managing complex infrastructure in the era of AI.

This static view slows down response times and leads to unexpected spending.

But instead of proactive alerts, legacy approaches to hybrid and multi-cloud management often leave organizations with so-called “dirty data” — outdated information from servers, VMs, and cloud systems.

And as more companies look to adopt AI, Infragraph will serve as the unified knowledge foundation to power trusted, automated workflows.

Impact

At IBM Think, we are excited to announce that HCP Terraform powered by Infragraph will be available to qualified US HCP Terraform customers in public preview. We’ll also detail how HCP Terraform customers can begin using this functionality now. How to get started with the public preview:  As announced at IBM Think 2026, if you are already an HCP Terraform customer, as of May 8, you will be able to access the Terraform powered by Infragraph public preview. Get started with Infragraph by  logging in to your HCP organization , then click the Infragraph tile to configure connections, set up queries, and add users.

Resolution / fix

Security patching and risk mitigation get harder, complexity grows, and costs escalate.

For example, when AI lets hackers target vulnerabilities with remarkable speed and precision, any security lapses must be discovered and patched in real-time.

Lessons learned

The problem isn’t getting better, especially as infrastructure changes happen more frequently, exploits materialize faster, and costs get out of control quicker.

Even with more powerful security and data capabilities, if data is dispersed, the company will still operate from behind when it comes to identifying, remediating, and preventing issues.
